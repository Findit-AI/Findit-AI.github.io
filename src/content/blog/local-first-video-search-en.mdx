Video teams lose more time finding clips than editing them.

FindIt is built around one baseline: search should feel immediate while media stays under your control.

## What local-first means in practice

Local-first is not a slogan. It changes daily behavior:

- Your core retrieval path is available even when your network is unstable.
- Your source footage does not need to be uploaded just to make search useful.
- Your library can travel with your drives and still remain usable.

## Why this matters for real teams

Editors and assistants often work under deadline pressure.  
In that context, reliability matters more than novelty.

A local-first model gives you predictable response patterns and fewer workflow interruptions.  
Cloud enhancement still has value, but it should enhance rather than gate the baseline.

## The product principle we keep

Free users should still get meaningful search value.  
Paid tiers should improve difficult semantic cases, not lock basic usefulness behind a paywall.

This keeps adoption low-risk and makes capability boundaries clear.
