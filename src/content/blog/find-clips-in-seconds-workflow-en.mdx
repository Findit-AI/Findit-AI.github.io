If your team already has enough footage, your bottleneck is usually retrieval, not creativity.

This workflow keeps handoff speed and result quality consistent.

## Step 1: Keep library structure predictable

Use one top-level folder per project or client, and avoid frequent renames mid-production.  
Stable paths make indexing and handoff more reliable.

## Step 2: Start with intent-first queries

Use short phrases with three parts:

- scene or subject
- action
- location or context

Example: `interview + walking + city street`

## Step 3: Validate quickly, then narrow

Begin with broad search.  
Once promising clips appear, narrow with additional terms instead of starting overly specific.

## Step 4: Export with context

When handing off results to editors:

- keep shared naming conventions
- include why each clip was selected
- store exports in consistent project folders

This avoids repeated discovery work later.

## Step 5: Review failed queries weekly

Run a short weekly review of failed searches to refine team query patterns.
